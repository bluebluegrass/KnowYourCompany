If you are a non-EU/EEA national considering working from Amsterdam, the relevant Dutch pathway would be the Kennismigrant (Highly Skilled Migrant) visa — but this requires your employer to be an IND-recognised sponsor (erkend referent).

No data was found confirming or denying whether Sardine (or SardineAI Europe UAB) is on the IND public register of recognised sponsors. The IND register should be checked directly at ind.nl to verify current status.

Given that Sardine's European office is in Lithuania and the careers page does not list Netherlands as a hiring region, it is unclear whether they would support or sponsor a Dutch work permit for a remote employee based in Amsterdam. This is a critical question to raise with the hiring team before proceeding.

What They Do

Sardine is an AI risk platform for fraud prevention, compliance, and credit underwriting. The platform combines device intelligence, behavior biometrics, and machine learning to screen transactions and logins in real time, help teams automate AML/KYC workflows, and stop financial crime before it happens. It is sold as a B2B SaaS product to fintechs, crypto companies, banks, and enterprise technology companies.

The company's stated mission: "Stop financial crime before it happens."

Offices: Miami, Florida (US HQ); North York, Ontario (Canada); Vilnius, Lithuania (Europe). Remote team across 15+ countries.
