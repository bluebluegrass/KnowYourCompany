9. Visa & Immigration Sponsorship Concern found
▼

Poki B.V. is not listed as a recognised sponsor on the IND Public Register for Regular Labour and Highly Skilled Migrants (last updated April 3, 2026).

In the Netherlands, companies must be registered with the IND (Immigration and Naturalisation Service) as a "recognised sponsor" in order to apply for a Highly Skilled Migrant (Kennismigrant) permit on behalf of a new employee. Without this status, a company cannot sponsor non-EU/EEA workers through the standard fast-track route.

  • The Content Analyst job posting explicitly requires candidates to be based in the Netherlands with regular on-site availability in Amsterdam. This language strongly implies the company expects applicants to already have the right to work in the Netherlands.
  • No mention of visa sponsorship, relocation support, or immigration assistance was found anywhere in Poki's public job postings or company materials.
  • Poki does not appear in myvisajobs.com or similar H-1B databases (these are US-specific and not applicable to a Dutch company).

What this means in practice: If you currently hold a valid work permit or have EU/EEA/Dutch citizenship, this is not an issue. If you require sponsorship (e.g., you are outside the Netherlands or the EU/EEA), this is a material blocker. You should raise this directly with Poki's recruitment team before investing time in the application process.

11. Company Profile & History No concerns
▼

What They Do

Poki operates the world's largest browser-based gaming platform. Players access thousands of free online games directly in a web browser — no download, no login required. The company monetises through advertising and shares a portion of ad revenue with the game developers it partners with.

Poki functions as both a marketplace and a publisher: it curates games, runs user testing infrastructure for developers, and actively helps developers improve games before and after launch. The platform serves 100 million players monthly across more than 100 countries in 34 languages.

Key Milestones

  • 2005: Michiel van Amerongen creates a personal web games site as a coding hobby project
  • 2013: Poki B.V. formally incorporated in Amsterdam; Sebastiaan Moeys joins as co-founder
  • 2014: Official launch of Poki.com as a cross-platform game publisher
  • 2014–2024: Grows from 10 million to 100 million monthly players without external investment
  • 2024: Amsterdam office renovated to accommodate team expansion; team reaches ~50 employees
  • June 2025: Hits 1 billion gameplays in a single month
  • 2025: Team grows to 65 employees; 625 million total players visit the platform during the year
  • December 2025: Wins Best in Business at the Dutch Game Awards
  • April 2026: Confirmed still bootstrapped; 100 million monthly active players

Headcount: Approximately 65 full-time employees (early 2026). Headquarters: Spui 10, 1012 WZ Amsterdam, Netherlands.
