11. Company Profile & History No concerns

What They Do

Sardine is an AI-powered risk platform for fraud, compliance, and credit — purpose-built for fintechs, banks, crypto platforms, and marketplaces. The platform consolidates fragmented data signals (device intelligence, behavioral biometrics, transaction patterns, identity data) into a unified risk-scoring engine to detect and prevent fraud, money laundering, and account abuse in real time.

Products include: instant ACH payment protection, account-opening fraud detection, AML transaction monitoring, case management, and the Sonar Consortium (a shared fraud intelligence network). The company's name is a reference to Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) — the regulatory filings used in financial crime compliance.

Key Milestones

  • February 2020: Founded by Soups Ranjan, Aditya Goel, and Zahid Shaikh — all former Revolut colleagues
  • February 2022: Series A ($19.5M, a16z). Team at approximately 50 clients by this point.
  • September 2022: Series B ($51.5M, a16z). Grew from 50 to 135+ clients in 7 months.
  • 2023: Named Forbes Next Billion Dollar Companies and LinkedIn Top 20 Startups. Became Nacha Preferred Partner.
  • 2024: 130% ARR growth; customer base doubled; 2.2 billion devices profiled
  • February 2025: Series C ($70M, Activant Capital). Total raised: $145M. Valuation ~$660M. 300+ enterprise customers across 70 countries.
  • 2025–2026: Team reaches 200+ employees across 15+ countries; Experian UK partnership announced

Headquarters: Miami, FL (US). Additional offices: North York, ON (Canada); Vilnius, Lithuania (Europe). Engineers and analysts are distributed globally.
